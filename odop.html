One District One Product

One District One Product (ODOP) initiative is aimed at fostering balanced regional development across all districts of the country. The initiative aims to select, brand, and promote at least One Product from each District (One District - One Product) of the country for enabling holistic socioeconomic growth across all regions. The ODOP Initiative has identified a total of 1102 products from 761 districts across the country.

Under the ODOP initiative, all products have been selected by States/UTs by taking into consideration the existing ecosystem on the ground, products identified under Districts as Export Hubs (DEH), and GI-tagged products. The finalized list is communicated to DPIIT by the relevant Department of States/UTs. All activities including exhibitions, capacity building, etc. are undertaken at the State/UT and district level, in consultation and coordination with the States/UTs.

ODOP Bihar

The Handicras of India through the ages fascinated the world by its creavity, aesthecs and exquisite crasmanship. It is a highly decentralized acvity, handicras of India present rich cultural heritage and shining example of using local resources, skills and iniaves. India's handicras are almost inexhausble with infinite variety which has been refined in course of changing history. The cross currents inspire the creave impulse of our crasmen. Moreover cras are results of years of unconscious experiment and evoluon; skills inherited and passed over generaons from forefathers to sons and grandsons.

Bihar is a state that is deeply connected to its great cultural heritage. The tradional art, architecture, countless artefacts make Bihar the beauful culturally rich state that it is. Bihar has been the main creator of handicras since the early period of human civilizaon. The crasmanship here was at its peak during the Maurya period. The erstwhile Pataliputra developed as a center of stone sculpture during the Maurya period. The creaon of terracoa had started in Bihar during the Maurya period. Aer independence, tradional handicras of Bihar surfaced and efforts were started to revive them. This increased their demand in the market.

Objectives

The objectives of the Programme are to:
-- Secure preservation and development of local crafts / skills and promotion of the art
-- Provide employment to youth and promote the competitive ecosystem in the state
-- Capacity building and promotion of local skills
-- Preventing migration by improving income and local employment in the state
-- Improvement in product quality and skill development
-- Increase overall exports of the selected products
-- Promote ODOP products to global level with the structured approach

Mission & Vision

One District One Product Programme is aimed at creating product-specific traditional industrial hubs across 75 districts of Uttar Pradesh that will promote traditional industries that are synonymous with the respective districts of the state.
